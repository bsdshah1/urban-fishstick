# Addition and Subtraction Calculation Policy

> **Note:** This calculation policy is primarily presented as a visual document in the original PDF. Each page contains diagrams, models, images, and worked examples demonstrating the progression of addition and subtraction methods from concrete representations through to abstract formal methods. The text extracted from the PDF consists largely of mathematical symbols (=, +, −) embedded within visual layouts.

## Overview

The policy follows the **Concrete–Pictorial–Abstract (CPA)** approach, showing the progression of addition and subtraction methods across year groups from EYFS through to Year 6.

### Key Progression Stages

**Concrete** — Children use physical objects and manipulatives (cubes, counters, number lines, base-10 equipment) to understand the operation.

**Pictorial** — Children use drawings and representations (bar models, part-whole models, number lines drawn on paper) to represent calculations.

**Abstract** — Children move to using numerals and formal written methods (column addition with and without exchange, column subtraction with and without exchange).

### Methods Covered (by stage)

| Stage | Addition Methods | Subtraction Methods |
|-------|-----------------|---------------------|
| EYFS / KS1 | Combining groups, counting on, number bonds, part-whole model | Taking away, counting back, finding the difference |
| Lower KS2 | Column addition (no exchange), column addition (with exchange) | Column subtraction (no exchange), column subtraction (with exchange) |
| Upper KS2 | Multi-digit column addition, mental strategies | Multi-digit column subtraction, efficient subtraction strategies |
