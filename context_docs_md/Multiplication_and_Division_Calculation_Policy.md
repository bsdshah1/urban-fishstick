# Multiplication and Division Calculation Policy

> **Note:** This calculation policy is primarily presented as a visual document in the original PDF. Each page contains diagrams, models, images, and worked examples demonstrating the progression of multiplication and division methods from concrete representations through to abstract formal methods. The text extracted from the PDF consists largely of mathematical symbols (×, ÷, =) embedded within visual layouts.

## Overview

The policy follows the **Concrete–Pictorial–Abstract (CPA)** approach, showing the progression of multiplication and division methods across year groups from EYFS through to Year 6.

### Key Progression Stages

**Concrete** — Children use physical objects (cubes, counters, arrays) to explore equal groups, sharing and grouping.

**Pictorial** — Children use drawings and representations (arrays, bar models, number lines) to represent multiplication and division.

**Abstract** — Children move to formal written methods (short multiplication, long multiplication, short division, long division).

### Methods Covered (by stage)

| Stage | Multiplication Methods | Division Methods |
|-------|----------------------|-----------------|
| EYFS / KS1 | Counting in equal groups, repeated addition, arrays | Sharing equally, grouping |
| Lower KS2 | Grid method, expanded written method, short multiplication | Sharing, grouping, short division (no remainder) |
| Upper KS2 | Short multiplication (multi-digit), long multiplication (up to 4-digit × 2-digit) | Short division (with remainder), long division |

### Times Table Expectations

Children's multiplication and division learning is supported by the Times Table Progression:

| Year Group | Focus |
|------------|-------|
| EYFS | Practical groupings |
| Year 1 | 2, 5, 10 |
| Year 2 | 2, 5, 10, 3 |
| Year 3 | 3, 4, 8, 6 |
| Year 4 | 7, 9, 11, 12 and all |
| Year 5–6 | All times tables |
