# Maths Vocabulary Progression

> The document maps key vocabulary across all strands of the mathematics curriculum (number, calculation, fractions, measurement, geometry, statistics), showing how vocabulary builds progressively across year groups to support pupils' mathematical communication and reasoning.

This document outlines the mathematical vocabulary pupils are expected to encounter, use, and apply from 

**EYFS to Year 6**.

It supports a mastery approach by promoting:
- consistent mathematical language across the school
- clear progression in vocabulary by year group
- improved reasoning through precise terminology
- movement from informal to formal mathematical language

## Purpose

Providing children with the correct mathematical vocabulary is a key part of effective mathematics teaching. Classroom displays, modelling, discussion, and written work should all reinforce this language.

This progression document is intended to:
- define expected vocabulary for each year group
- support consistent teaching across classes
- strengthen pupils’ mathematical communication
- help pupils explain methods, justify reasoning, and make connections

## How to Use This Document

- Introduce vocabulary explicitly during teaching.
- Revisit previously taught vocabulary regularly.
- Model correct mathematical language in discussion and written explanations.
- Ensure classroom displays reflect current and prior learning.
- Encourage pupils to use precise vocabulary when reasoning.

## Progression by Year Group

### EYFS
Vocabulary is introduced through practical exploration, counting, comparison, shape, measure, and positional language.

**Typical strands include:**
- number
- comparison
- addition and subtraction
- shape
- measure
- position and direction

### Year 1
Vocabulary expands to support early calculation, place value, shape, measure, and simple fractions.

**Typical strands include:**
- number and place value
- addition and subtraction
- multiplication and division
- fractions
- measurement
- geometry

### Year 2
Vocabulary is extended through larger numbers, formal methods, data handling, and more precise fraction and measure language.

**Typical strands include:**
- number and place value
- addition and subtraction
- multiplication and division
- fractions
- measurement
- statistics
- geometry

### Year 3
Vocabulary becomes more formal as pupils work with larger numbers, written methods, time, measure, fractions, and properties of shape.

**Typical strands include:**
- number and place value
- calculation
- fractions
- measurement
- geometry
- statistics

### Year 4
Pupils consolidate formal mathematical language across all key strands and begin using more technical vocabulary in geometry, decimals, and data.

**Typical strands include:**
- number and place value
- addition and subtraction
- multiplication and division
- fractions and decimals
- measurement
- geometry
- statistics

### Year 5
Vocabulary broadens to include more complex number concepts, negative numbers, factors, multiples, decimals, percentages, and more advanced geometry.

**Typical strands include:**
- number and place value
- calculation
- fractions, decimals, and percentages
- measurement
- geometry
- statistics

### Year 6
Vocabulary prepares pupils for secondary mathematics through increasingly precise use of language linked to ratio, algebra, advanced fractions, formal calculation, and geometry.

**Typical strands include:**
- number and place value
- calculation
- fractions, decimals, and percentages
- ratio
- algebra
- measurement
- geometry
- statistics
